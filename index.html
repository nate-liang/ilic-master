About ILIC

The premier undergraduate stock pitch competition in the Ivy League.

The Brown Investment Group is launching the Ivy League Investment Competition — a first-of-its-kind, invite-only undergraduate equity research competition bringing together the top investment talent across all eight Ivy League institutions.

ILIC will convene 8 elite teams to present institutional-quality investment ideas to a panel of leading investors, hedge funds, and asset managers. The event is designed to become the premier undergraduate stock pitch competition in the Ivy League — a high-signal recruiting and talent discovery opportunity for participating firms.

Invite-Only

No open registration. Each school submits 2 pitches; Brown × Silver Beech selects 1. Eight elite teams total.

Institutional-Quality Research

Full equity research report, financial model, valuation, and variant perception vs. consensus.

A High-Signal Environment

Firms evaluate candidates through real investment thinking, not resumes. Confirmed sponsors include Bridgewater and BlackRock.

November 2026 · Providence, RI

Two days at Brown University. Travel reimbursement, one night of housing, and meals covered for all participants.

Team Eligibility

Who can compete.

ILIC aggregates the top undergraduate investors across all Ivy League schools into a single, high-intensity environment — leaders of student-run investment funds, top performers in highly selective investment clubs, and candidates targeting buy-side and top-tier finance roles.

Teams are selected by invitation only. No open registration.

Competition Format

Three rounds. One champion.

A three-round format designed to surface the best investment thinking — not just the best presentation.

Round I
School Selection
  • Each school submits 2 pitches
  • Full equity research report required
  • Financial model & valuation included
→ Brown × Silver Beech selects 1 per school
Round II
Preliminary Pitches
  • 8 teams pitch — 2 rooms, 2 judges each
  • 20 minutes per pitch, live judging
  • Held at Brown University, Day 1
→ Top 4 teams advance to finals
Round III
Live Finals
  • 4 finalist teams present on Day 2
  • 30 minutes per team, full judge panel
  • 20-minute Q&A after each pitch
→ Winners announced at final lunch
Evaluation Criteria
Depth & Rigor of Research Originality & Investment Insight Valuation Discipline Risk Assessment Presentation Quality
